Aircraft Specifications & History

A Legacy of Performance

The Piper PA-24 Comanche is a family of four-seat, single-engined, low-wing airplanes that were produced by Piper Aircraft from 1957 to 1972. The Comanche 250, introduced in 1958, was known for its excellent performance, efficiency, and structural integrity. With its distinctive stabilator (a single-piece horizontal stabilizer and elevator), it became a popular choice for both personal use and as a cross-country touring aircraft, setting numerous speed and distance records in its class.
